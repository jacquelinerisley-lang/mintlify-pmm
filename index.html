Executive summary

Mintlify has earned its position as the default documentation platform for developer-first startups — but the opportunity in front of it is structurally different. The rise of AI agents as first-class consumers of product knowledge has created a new category that no incumbent owns and no analyst has yet named. That category is AI Knowledge Infrastructure, and Mintlify is best positioned to define it.

This document outlines a three-part strategy for making that transition. First, a disciplined approach to category creation — establishing the vocabulary, building analyst relationships, and earning formal recognition on a realistic 18–30 month timeline. Second, a cross-functional model for capturing enterprise interest through product launches and campaigns that connect Product, Demand, and Sales around a consistent message. Third, the sales enablement infrastructure needed to help an enterprise-facing Sales team speak credibly to a more skeptical, more senior buyer: CPOs, CTOs, VPs of Engineering, and Directors of Developer Experience.

These three pillars are not independent workstreams. Every analyst briefing sharpens the enterprise positioning. Every enterprise win gives analysts a proof point. Every sales call surfaces the objections that improve the messaging. The strategy is designed to compound — and the 30·60·90 plan at the end of this document shows how it starts.

The situation

Where Mintlify is

The default docs platform for developer-first startups. 100M+ developers a year, 20,000+ customers, trusted by Anthropic, Coinbase, Perplexity. Product-led, beloved by EPD teams.

Where Mintlify is going

Knowledge infrastructure for the enterprise. From helping startups publish docs to helping organizations make their product knowledge available to every AI system that matters.

The market window

No analyst category exists yet for AI knowledge infrastructure. Nearly half of all docs traffic is now AI agents. The category is forming — and Mintlify can define it before anyone else does.

01

Category creation

How to earn a named spot in a Gartner or Forrester report.

Mintlify needs to own the strategic narrative for elevating "AI Knowledge Infrastructure" from a documentation platform or publishing tool that is a component of larger solutions to a critical system layer.
Core strategic premise

What analysts need to create a category worthy of an MQ or Wave

Requirement 01

A defensible category definition

Analysts cover markets, not features. The category needs a clear problem statement and a specific set of capabilities that define what is in and what is out. "AI Knowledge Infrastructure" frames Mintlify as the system layer — not the publishing tool — and must be defined tightly enough that analysts can apply it consistently across vendors.

Requirement 02

Enterprise revenue signals

Gartner covers markets where enterprises are making large, standalone investments. Named enterprises with significant contracts — outside of bundled platform plays — are the primary signal that a category has real buying behavior behind it. Without this, analysts see a feature, not a market.

Requirement 03

5–7 viable named vendors

Analysts don't build reports for a single company. There must be at least 5–7 vendors offering a product that fits the category definition and winning meaningful enterprise deals. This means Mintlify benefits from naming the category broadly enough to include adjacent players — while positioning itself firmly at the top.

MQs and Waves are not realistic or desirable short-term (1–2 year) goals

🔍

Analysts currently misunderstand the space

Gartner and Forrester currently see AI Knowledge Infrastructure as a subset or component of a larger framework — not a standalone category. Appearing in an MQ or Wave before that framing shifts could cement the wrong perception and be used to minimize Mintlify's position.

📉

A small dot in the lower left does more harm than good

MQ and Wave placement requires broad capabilities and global revenue scale. A low placement would be used by competitors and skeptical buyers to discredit Mintlify at exactly the moment it is trying to establish category leadership. Timing matters as much as inclusion.

💼

Analysts are prioritizing consulting over exhaustive reports

Gartner and Forrester are shifting focus toward their more profitable consulting businesses. New MQs and Waves require overwhelming market signal to justify the investment. The smarter near-term path is building the relationships and data that make Mintlify the obvious choice when that report eventually gets created.

Realistic short-term goals and how to measure them (within ~1 year)

Presence & visibility

  • Get first-page listing on Gartner Peer Reviews to demonstrate market presence to analysts
  • Appear in a Voice of the Customer Report to demonstrate market leadership
  • Use inquiries to identify opportunities to be included in market reports and niche overviews
  • Gain credibility to be featured in Cool Vendor or Hype Cycle reports

Influence & relationship

  • Provide such strong content and insights during analyst interactions that we inspire the creation of shorter-form reports on AI Knowledge Infrastructure
  • Create owned research analysts can use as a primary reference point — an annual "State of AI Knowledge Infrastructure" report with original data on AI vs. human traffic patterns
  • Document and track instances of analysts discussing Mintlify in customer conversations (measurable through your account rep)

Timeline to analyst coverage

Now → Month 3

Seed the language

Brief key analysts on the category vision (not the product) and provide data from Mintlify's experience to support it. Get "AI knowledge infrastructure" into 5+ tier-1 tech media pieces and amplify with SEO/AEO and social media to establish the vocabulary before competitors do. Analyst briefings are free — they want to hear from category creators.

Month 3 → Month 9

Build the analyst relationship

Quarterly updates to priority analysts at Gartner and Forrester. Invite them to customer advisory board sessions. Provide unpublished data from Mintlify's traffic analytics. Use inquiries to solidify the relationship and gain knowledge that can enhance product, sales, and marketing efforts. Sponsor an analyst webinar on the AI agent experience — co-branded, not a commercial. First analyst mentions typically appear in passing coverage of adjacent categories.

Month 9 → Month 18

Enterprise proof points

Name enterprise customers in press. Get 3+ customers to participate in analyst reference calls. Hit $5M ARR from deals > $50K. Qualify for Gartner Peer Insights listing for the category. At this stage, Mintlify starts showing up in inquiry responses when enterprises ask analysts "what should we use for knowledge infrastructure" — and analysts begin proactively recommending knowledge infrastructure to their clients. Track this through your account rep.

Month 18 → Month 30

First formal report inclusion

The realistic path to a Gartner Market Guide (the precursor to a Magic Quadrant) is 18–24 months from first briefing. A Forrester Wave requires broader market maturity — more likely at 24–36 months. The goal isn't to rush a quadrant; it's to be the reference point when analysts write the first "note" on the category, which typically happens within 18 months of consistent engagement.

02

Capture interest

How Product Marketing collaborates with Product, Demand, and Sales to convert market movement into pipeline.

Launch

Product Marketing acts as the central point for launch operations — establishing sustainable, cross-functional processes and tiered checklists for Tier 1, 2, and 3 launches. Below is how that partnership plays out across each team.

🔧
Product

Product feedback loop

Attend sprint reviews and partner with product management to powerfully communicate how new features translate into customer benefits. Synthesize market research, analyst insights, and customer feedback to inform user stories, features, and new products.

Thought leadership

By understanding the vision of executive and product leadership, develop thought leadership that captures the attention of CPOs, VPs of Engineering, CTOs, and Product Ops — a highly effective tool at the launch moment.

Education

With input from the product team, create how-tos, proprietary research, and other assets that capture tech buyer interest and establish Mintlify's credibility in the space.

User proof points

Identify beta users who can add credibility to launches with quotes, testimonials, event and webinar participation, analyst references, and case studies.

Analyst relations

Senior product leadership participation in analyst launch briefings adds significant credibility — especially when we can demonstrate a compelling long-term vision for the category.

📣
Demand

Messaging frameworks

Provide a launch messaging framework that translates new features into user benefits — a blueprint for all related inbound and outbound demand gen across the funnel.

Strategic partnership

Partner on campaigns and channels to promote thought leadership and education tied to the launch, ensuring consistent narrative across every touchpoint.

Hands-on execution

Write launch announcements, social media, press releases, persona and funnel-based emails, and blog posts. Or act as a timely reviewer on Demand-generated materials — whatever the team determines will create the biggest moment.

Video & webinar production

Produce prospect-facing webinars or videos focused on the benefits of new functionality — ideally featuring a beta customer. Produce separate customer-facing content to ensure existing users understand new features and enterprise value.

Website alignment

Determine need for website changes — homepage splash for major updates, targeted content changes on product pages for incremental launches.

External updates

Ensure external sites like G2 and Gartner Peer Reviews reflect the most up-to-date product functionality — these sites generate inbound demand and directly influence enterprise buyers.

💼
Sales

Preparation

Sales training covering business benefits of new features, competitive analysis with talking points, objection handling, and sales messaging frameworks — calibrated to launch tier.

Quick references

Easy-to-use summary materials that AEs can reach for instantly in the heat of a deal — competitive snapshot, top objections, key proof points. See examples below.

Sales playbook

Robust guidance on sales actions along with product briefs for TOFU and BOFU. Email messaging for sequences at different stages of the sales cycle, ready to drop into CRM.

Post-sales

Customer Success training, assets, and playbooks to position new features for expansion, retention, and upsell — especially where there are opportunities to move from departmental to enterprise solutions.

In-person support

Join sales calls to answer business-level questions about new capabilities or give brief presentations and demos where the deal warrants it.

Campaigns

As Mintlify complements successful PLG with an enterprise focus, Product Marketing leads the way in sharpening messaging, refining personas, and identifying promising market segments for campaign execution.

🔧
Product

Validation

Partner with PM to validate technical accuracy, confirm value propositions, and identify customer proof points that anchor campaign claims in real evidence.

Data

Gather user and product data that can support campaign claims — average time a process takes, how many users run a given workflow, performance benchmarks — and translate them into compelling market-facing proof.

Primary source material

Internal data can generate the "State of the Industry" reports and original research that form the backbone of a whole campaign — spanning social, blog posts, webinar presentations, and more. Mintlify's traffic data is uniquely valuable here.

📣
Demand

End-to-end campaign support

Whether initiated by Demand, Marketing Leadership, or Product Marketing — provide and review the messaging framework, participate in creation of inbound and outbound content across the full funnel, help secure expert voices (customers, analysts, internal SMEs) for webinars and events, and refine personas and target markets.

Identify opportunities

Brainstorm and submit proposals for campaigns that will open enterprise opportunities — grounded in interactions with customers, analysts, and findings from sales call analysis and competitive research.

💼
Sales

Sales activation

Provide an overview of campaign positioning, updated objection handling, and new content — so AEs can activate the campaign narrative in their outreach from day one.

Robust campaign playbook

Deliver a full campaign playbook including: campaign overview (personas targeted, messaging, channels, assets); complete product and sales messaging frameworks; competitive intel table; assets tagged by stage, company size, and target market; and email templates integrated with CRM sequencing. Support with in-person attendance at customer meetings and events where high-value deals are at stake.

Example 1 · Sales messaging framework
A cheat-sheet-style messaging framework delivered to Sales at Aravo when a new Information Security capability launched. Designed as a refresher within a broader training context — fast to scan, easy to use in a live deal.

Sales messaging for Information Security

Before Scenario

  • •Manually scoping, distributing, and tracking SIG questionnaires was complicated, time consuming, and inefficient
  • •Reporting on overall information security risk was difficult due to the need to compile multiple spreadsheets
  • •No centralized view of overall information security risk to ensure that standards and controls are adhered to

No visibility into overall 3rd party infosec risk

After Scenario

  • •Risk expert can quickly and easily scope the appropriate assessment using SIG Core and/or Lite
  • •Automated workflow manages associated business processes, reducing manual processes
  • •Centralized view of infosec risk with pre-configured reporting and dashboards

Complete visibility into 3rd party information management risk across the enterprise

Requirements

  • •Adhere to industry-standard standard for information security assessments (Shared Assessments SIG)
  • •Identify and mitigate potential infosec risks without creating undue burden on vendors or employees
  • •Demonstrate appropriate levels of due diligence and reporting to senior management, the board, and auditors

Negative Consequences

  • •Reputational risk has cost publicly traded companies more than $180 billion in the past several years
  • •The average cost of a data breach is $4 million (Ponemon Institute)
  • •87% of consumers will stop doing business with a company they don't trust with their data (PWC)
  • •EU GDPR, FTC, CFPB, various states, and other authorities have issued fines to organizations that failed to protect customer/employee data from third party breaches

Positive Business Impact

  • •Improve decision making by identifying potential risks associated with vendors that access customer/employee data
  • •Reduce the amount of time and effort it takes to confidently onboard a new vendor
  • •Protect and preserve brand/reputation and shareholder value

Proof Points

  • •Target paid out more than $18.5 million in fines to 47 states and DC for the 2013 breach caused by an HVAC vendor (total costs estimated at $200 million)
  • •A third-party data breach at Evite (2019) exposed data of up to 100 million users, creating GDPR exposure for every organization that used the service
  • •American Medical Collection Agency (AMCA) and Quest Diagnostics are facing millions in lawsuits and potential HIPAA fines after data related to 12 million customers may have been exposed

Discovery Questions

  • •What is your role in assessing IT vendors? (CISO, IT security, etc.)
  • •What is your current process for assessing IT vendor risk?
  • •Do you currently use the SIG to assess vendors?
  • •How long would it take you to assess a third party's information security risk?
  • •How long would it take you to report on the inherent information security risk for your entire third-party portfolio?

Metrics

  • •63% of data breaches are caused by actions of a third party (Ponemon Institute)
  • •The number of confirmed data breaches caused by third parties increased 17% between 2017 and 2018 (Forrester)
  • •66% of organizations have experienced a third-party data breach in the past year (Ponemon)

Product Marketing responsibilities

🎤

Field & event support

Support the Sales team at events and on sales calls — especially in the early enterprise motion where Product Marketing can help contextualize the broader category narrative for skeptical EPD buyers.

Provide timely training and updates on new launches, market activity, campaigns, and anything else that impacts how Sales interacts with prospects or customers. No AE should be caught flat-footed by a market event PMM already knows about.

Objection handling matrix

Objection What's behind it The response
"We already use Confluence / Notion." Incumbent inertia; they haven't felt the AI-era pain yet. "Confluence and Notion are built for humans finding information. They're not built for AI agents consuming it at scale. When a developer asks Claude how to use your API, Claude isn't reading Confluence — and if it is, it's paying a massive token tax on all that formatting noise. Mintlify is the layer that makes your knowledge machine-readable, wherever it currently lives."
"We built our own docs system / we have engineering resources." Not-invented-here, or genuine concern about migration effort. "The teams that built their own docs infrastructure before Mintlify existed all made the same calculation: it's a docs site, how hard can it be? The ones who switched tell us they were spending 2–3 engineers maintaining infrastructure instead of shipping product. The question isn't 'can we build it' — it's 'should an engineering team be maintaining a docs platform in 2026 when AI knowledge infrastructure is becoming a strategic asset?'"
"We're not sure our team will actually keep docs updated." A legitimate fear — they've seen documentation decay before. "That was the right concern for docs platforms. Mintlify's Workflows solve this differently: when your product changes, Mintlify drafts the documentation update automatically, flags it for review, and maintains the knowledge without requiring engineers to context-switch. The docs stay accurate because the system keeps them accurate, not because humans remember to."
"How is this different from GitBook / Readme?" Need a clear differentiator beyond aesthetics. "GitBook and Readme are documentation publishers. Mintlify is knowledge infrastructure. That distinction matters because your docs are no longer just a website — they're the interface through which AI systems understand your product. Mintlify is the only platform built from the ground up to serve both humans browsing a site and AI agents consuming content programmatically. That's llms.txt, MCP server support, machine-readable Markdown exports, and self-updating content — none of which GitBook or Readme are architected to do."
"Security / compliance concerns for enterprise." Real requirement, not just a blocker tactic. "Mintlify is SOC 2 Type II certified, in process for ISO 27001 and GDPR, and supports SAML-based SSO with granular access controls. We have dedicated enterprise migration support and white-glove onboarding. [Fidelity / AT&T / enterprise name] went through the same security review — here's the security one-pager and we can schedule a call with our security team directly."

Sales enablement tools

Purpose-built sales enablement platform

Strategic investment

Enterprise EPD buyers are more skeptical and more influenced by educational and thought leadership content at the top of the funnel — but still require tightly written product content to make a final decision. Managing this asset ecosystem effectively requires a purpose-built enablement platform, such as Highspot, Seismic, or DocSend.

Playbook activation

Supports structured playbook delivery — AEs work through the right assets at the right stage automatically, rather than hunting for the latest version of a deck.

Smart asset surfacing

AEs or Demand can quickly identify the right asset for any prospect based on company size, ICP, target market, product, or sales stage — without leaving CRM.

CRM integration

Automates asset delivery to prospects and gives AEs access to the full content library from within the tools they already live in.

Prospect behavior data

Tracks whether a prospect opened an asset, read or watched the whole thing, and shared it with a colleague — signal that fundamentally changes how AEs follow up.

Asset performance analytics

Surfaces which assets are driving engagement and which aren't — keeping Product Marketing accountable and ensuring the library stays lean and effective.

Content governance

Ensures AEs are always working from the most current content — no more outdated decks being sent to prospects because someone forgot to update a shared Drive folder.
